23 Best Places For 2 Days Trip In India

  • 23 Best Places For 2 Days Trip In India

India is a country that is replete with historical and natural wonders. The topography of the land varies probably after every few hundred kilometres. If you find enchanting deserts, you will also find places with ample greenery, if there are hot sunny beaches then there are also some majestic snow-clad mountains. However, the work culture in India may demand a lot of time from you and hamper your leisure activities. Many a time, you may also face a financial crunch and cannot afford a long holiday tour.

To help you ease the stress, we bring to you all the best places for 2 days trip in India. Yes, it is true. It is possible to travel for a weekend in India and that too on a budget. Moreover, it is not just limited to one or two cities, but throughout the country. These incredible short package tours are certain to take you on an expedition to India in an extraordinarily impressive way. So scroll down and read all about the places for 2 days trip in India that you could opt for.

1. Goa

Enjoy amidst the shining golden sands, swaying palms and sparkling waters when you choose Goa among the other places for 2 days trip in India. The combination of clear blue skies and the azure Arabian Sea is a sight for sore eyes. This part of the western coast of the Indian Peninsula boasts of thrilling adventure sports such as banana boat ride, jet skiing, parasailing and more. Moreover, the nightlife in Goa is equally thriving with its nightclubs and beachside shacks.

Best Time To Visit:
November to March
Major Sightseeing Spots:
Chapora river and fort, Basilica of Bom Jesus, Dudhsagar Falls, Naval Aviation Museum, Fort Aguada, Calangute Beach

2. Pondicherry

As soon as the cold winds hit the south of India, do not think twice and set off to explore this small French town in India. It is one of the best places for 2 days trip in India. The otherwise hot and scorching sun in the state of Tamil Nadu hampers travel plans. Hence, in winters, the climate is cool and pleasant and you can make the most of it. One of the seven Union Territories of India, Pondicherry or Puducherry is an amalgamation of the conventional Indian sensibilities and French architecture. There are many authentic French bakeries and beer pubs to give you a feel of having landed straight in Paris.

Best Time To Visit: 
October to February

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Sri Aurobindo Ashram Pondicherry, Promenade Beach, Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Jesus, French War Memorial, Raj Nivas

3. Rishikesh

Set on the foothills of the mighty Himalayas, Rishikesh, in a nutshell, is the hub of several ancient temples, yoga ashrams, popular cafes and extreme adventure sports. The town has been a magnet for spiritual seekers. It stands as the Yoga Capital of the World and hence, the peace and tranquillity you will find here is unmatched. Moreover, if you have an adventure gene in you, there are several sports you can indulge in such as hiking, bungee jumping, trekking and white water river rafting. It is the best option for places for 2 days trip in India.

Best Time To Visit:
March to May and Mid September to November

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Lakshman Jhula, Parmarth Niketan Ashram, Rishikesh River Rafting, Beatles Ashram, Ram Jhula

4. Sikkim

Sikkim is that holiday destination where the photographers, adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts flock all over the region. It is the one-stop solution for your query of places for 2 days trip in India. This small town can be easily covered in 2-3 days and you can cover all its sightseeing places at ease. You should witness the suns rays levitate over Mt. Kanchenjunga, the third highest peak in the world.

Best Time To Visit:
March to May and also October to December

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Lake Tsomgo, Rumtek Monastery, Yumthang Valley, Gurudongmar Lake, Pemayangtse Monastery

5. Hampi

The first settlement dates back to 1st century AD, which stands proof of the long-standing history it has. Hampi is the city of ruins and is recognised as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. It is safe to call the city an open museum with over a hundred locations to explore. It is also a significant religious place as it houses the historic Virupaksha Temple, many other Jain temples and a monolithic idol of Lord Narasimha. It is one of the unique places for 2 days trip in India.

Best Time To Visit:
October to February

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Virupaksha Temple, Hampi Bazaar Street, Hampi Archaeological Ruins, Elephant Stables, Lotus Mahal Hampi

6. Lonavala

A trip to Lonavala and rains go in hand in hand. It is indeed among the best places to visit during monsoon for 2 days since it has many attractions to its name which all can be easily paid a visit to in 2-3 days. A world-class entertainment and theme park is also recently opened which is located at only at a few kilometres distance from here. Make sure to visit the Lonavala market that is studded with petit shops.

Best Time To Visit: 
June to January

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Bushi Dam, Lion Point, Tungarli Lake, Sunils Celebrity Wax Museum, Rajmachi Point

7. Gokarna

One of the new and emerging favourite beach towns on the western coast, Gokarna has some of the best beaches youll ever see in India. A number of beach cafs let you sip on chilled beers while enjoying the sea views. You can embark on a beach trek, a concept that is unusual in the rest of the country. It gets you through the beaches and into the dense forests. You follow the sound of waves and make it to the next sandy strip. It is one the best getaway places for 2 days trip in India.

Best Time To Visit:
October to March

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Om Beach, Mahabaleshwar Temple, Kudle Beach, Paradise Beach, Shiva cave

8. Udaipur

It is the crown jewel of the desert state of Rajasthan. It is also known as the Venice of the East owing to the many natural and man-made lakes it boasts of. With visits to hordes of forts, museums, lakes, monuments and palaces, you can experience the rich and cultural heritage of the Mewar reign. The grand structures of Udaipur such as the City Palace, Sajjangarh Fort, the many havelis, etc. gives it its distinctive royal charm. Travel internally to every site is easy as it is set close by. As the sun sets, take a boat ride across Fateh Sagar or Lake Pichola and see the glittering reflection of the shores in the citys lake waters.

Best Time To Visit:
September to March

Major Sightseeing Spots:
City Palace, Lake Pichola, Saheliyon-ki-Bari, Jagdish Temple, Bagore-ki-Haveli

9. Munnar

One of the best places to visit during summer for 2 days in India is this beautiful green city of Munnar. Being set on elevated land, it enjoys pleasant weather throughout the year and you can escape the burning summers of India. You will feel connected to nature directly and the lush green tea plantations will leave you awe-inspired. When in Munnar, you must visit the tea museums that houses photographs of ancient tea plucking processes and present-day machinery that contribute to thriving tea industry. An evening spent boating in the Mattupetty Lake will give you the most serene experience.

Best Time To Visit:
March to August

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Eravikulam National Park, Mattupetty Dam, Attukad Waterfall, C.S.I Christ Church, Anamudi

10. Jaipur

Jaipur is jam-packed with a colourful crowd. It makes the best choice on the list of places for 2 days trip in India. A beautiful pink hue spreads throughout the city and hence, it is called the pink city. You will come across several old forts, deserts and temples, each holding its unique and distant charm. Apart from the monuments, forts and palaces, make sure to enjoy mouth-watering Rajasthani thali at Chowki Dhani. Many budget hotels are easily available which will still have you feel the luxurious life of the Rajasthani royals.

Best Time To Visit:
October to March

Major Sightseeing Spots:
City Palace, Hawa Mahal, Amber Palace, Hawa Mahal, Jantar Mantar

11. McLeodganj

The stunning valleys and clean climate of McLeodganj make it for a perfect getaway with friends, family or your better half. It is one of the best hill stations in Kerala and the atmosphere will have you unwinding and relaxing in no time. It comprises of the gurgling streams, snow-capped mountains, rich cultural institutions and ancient monasteries. The sunset views here cannot be missed.

Best Time To Visit: 
June to September

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Naddi Lake, Dal Lake, Triund, Kareri Lake, Kangra Fort

12. Darjeeling

Darjeeling is the most sort-after destination in the country. This small town is a place best explored on feet as you walk through its colourful cobbled streets. Nestled in the foothills of the eastern Himalayas, Mother Nature has blessed Darjeeling with exceptional beauty. It is most popular for being a fine example of housing a mix of cultural and natural heritage.

Best Time To Visit: 
October to March

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Batasia Loop, Ghum Monastery, Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park, Peace Pagoda

13. Kanyakumari

Ride on an elephants back, get acquainted with villagers and enjoy many such things that resonate the spirit of traditional South India when you visit Kanyakumari. It is a vibrant amalgamation of the new and the old. Most of the restaurants around serve lip-smacking seafood which is an absolute delight for non-vegetarians. It gives you an opportunity to unwind in the lap of nature along with exploring the historic and cultural heritage of India.

Best Time To Visit: 
March to May

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Vivekananda Rock Memorial, Thiruvalluvar Statue, Kanyakumari Beach, Arulmigu Bhagavathy Amman Temple

14. Nainital

It is a dreamy escape that offers a chance to unwind and relax. Stroll down to the stunning lakeside promenades and hear the waves hit the shores. You can undertake a biking tour and explore all its by lanes. When you have only a few days at hand, Nanitals charm will help you separate from the citys clutter in no time.

Best Time To Visit: 
March to June

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Nainital Lake, G B Pant High Altitude Zoo, Cave Garden, Hanuman Garhi

15. Varanasi

Prepare to witness the grand Ganga aarti that will leave you mesmerised. The many temples and ghats in Varanasi will have you learn about the innumerable aspects of Hinduism. Just sit back at some caf on the riverfront and bask in the surroundings of the city that is bounded by hills on three sides. You can buy wonderfully made wooden figurines, colourful trinkets and a variety of stuff made out of cane from Varanasis markets.

Best Time To Visit: 
November to February

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Shri Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Dashashwamedh Ghat, Ramnagar Fort, Chaukhandi Stupa, Assi ghat

16. Meghalaya

Meghalaya is among the best places to visit in India for 2 days owing to the number of waterfalls, opulent greenery around and booming ponds and lakes. There are various sightseeing spots here and the misty hill station attracts many travellers every year. It becomes a wonderful decision owing to the fact that it is not yet commercialised and is less crowded. It is also slowly gaining popularity due to the number of adventurous activities it has in store for travellers.

Best Time To Visit: 
March to June

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Nohkalikai Falls, Umiam Lake, Shillong View Point, Don Bosco Museum, living root bridges

17. Dehradun

Among the places to visit in India for 2 days, Dehradun lets you enjoy your space and you will feel that a weekend is more than enough to rejuvenate your mind, body and soul. The nearby peaks, green valleys, verdant forests and glittering rivers are enough to keep you entranced and excited on your trip.

Best Time To Visit: 
March to June

Major Sightseeing Spots:
George Everests House, Tapkeshwar Temple, Rajaji National Park, Mindrolling Monastery

18. Pushkar

A haven for history lovers and architecture buffs, Pushkar is a must visit at least once in your life. Bounded by a number of ancient monuments, bustling street markets, beautiful temples, treasury building, bastions and captivating remains of Mewar and Mughal Empire, this small town in Rajasthan is a backpackers delight. You can plan to visit at least 2-3 sightseeing places a day and also partake in devouring Rajasthani cuisine comprising of dal bati churma, ghewar and many such traditional dishes.

Best Time To Visit: 
November to March

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Pushkar Lake, Rangji Temple, Lake Foy Sagar, Pushkar Cattle Fair, Brahma Ghat

19. Coorg

There are many architectural splendours in Coorg to see comprising of ancient churches and old-style bungalows that adds to the magnificent scenic beauty of the town. When you visit Coorg, make sure to devour seafood, which is absolutely scrumptious. This little hill station is a decision that you will never ever regret. This one of the amazing places for 2 days trip in India. 

Best Time To Visit: 
October to March

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Rajas Seat, Madikeri Fort, Abbey Falls Madkeri, Omkareshwar Temple, View Point Park

20. Agra

Do you ever crave to go back in time and live through the royalty of the Mughals? Agra is one such city that helps you fulfil this desire. It is home to what is one of the seven wonders of the world- The Taj Mahal. While you may think it is not possible to cover all in 2-3 days, you can choose the best places that give you enough insights on the citys culture and heritage.

Best Time To Visit: 
August to October

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, Jama Mosque, Taj Museum

21. Kasol

Kasol is the charming little village situated in the state of Himachal Pradesh in India. It is heaven for trekkers, backpackers, nature lovers and adventurous people. It is also known as The Amsterdam of India. The smooth flowing of the river along with the backdrop of the snow-capped mountains make it a magical place to visit. There are many places for 2 days trip in Kasol. It is a hub for those who wish to dwell in the lap of nature.

Best Time To Visit: 
October to February

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Kheer Ganga, Parvati River, Rasol, Tosh, Malana, Buddha Palace, Pulga Village, Naggar village, Chalal.

22. Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer is situated in amidst the vast and famous That desert in Rajasthan. It is also known as the Golden City because of its Yellowstone architecture. The name Jaisalmer is derived from Maharaj Jaisal Singh. This place has magnificent landscapes, amazing architecture, and historical buildings. If you are wondering what to see in Jaisalmer, there are many things to do in Jaisalmer like adventurous sports, fantastic safaris, scenic camping and many more. The temperature ranges from 12 degree Celsius to 28 degree Celsius. This is one of the best places for 2 days trip in India.

Best Time To Visit: 
November to march

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Jaisalmer fort, Gadisar lake, Shopping in Jaisalmer local bazaar, Kanoi, dune bashing in Thar desert, Camping in desert and desert safaris, desert national park, Jaisalmer war museum.

23. Shimla

Shimla is the hill station with vibrant colonial history. Situated at the height of 2200 meters. This is the most preferred place for the honeymoon and also for the family. It is a perfect getaway places for 2 days trip in India. The city of Shimla has beautiful architecture and long-standing churches. It is always covered by snow.

Best Time To Visit: 
October to February is the best time for snow lovers

Major Sightseeing Spots:
Mall Road, Kufri, Jakhoo temple, Christ Church, Cafe Simla Times, green Valley, The Indian Institute of Advanced Studies, Ridge.
